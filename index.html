Beyond Supersize Me, A Look at The Numbers



﹀

This is an exploration of which popular menu item would have the best cost/benefit ratio

Cost/benefit is relative as we live different lives and have different requirements.

The data is based on McDonald's 2017 nutritional facts

Sourced from: https://www.kaggle.com/mcdonalds/nutrition-facts

Each menu item was high in sodium, and had a high calorie from fat ratio. The Baked Apple Pie and Medium French Fries had less than 1g of cholesterol, which was surprising.

When put into the context of % of Daily Value, the real impact to our bodies is more clear. On a sidenote, There are 68 million people eating at a McDonald's everyday.

Think about calories in another way.

If you were a 140 lbs person, who can run a 10 min/mile, this is a breakdown of how much running you'd have to do to burn off the calories of each popular menu item.

Based on: calories, calories from fat, sodium, cholesterol, % daily values, serving size, and personal preferences
